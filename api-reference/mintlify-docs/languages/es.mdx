## Available Rules

### üìÖ Dates and Times

Control how dates, times, and temporal expressions are formatted in your translations.

**Rules available:** 3

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Date Format">
Control how dates are displayed (month/day/year vs day/month/year, etc.).

**Available options:**
1. **`use-numerals-only-with-leading-zero-for-single-digits`** - Numerals only with leading zero for single digits
2. **`use-numerals-only-without-leading-zero-for-single-digits`** - Numerals only without leading zero for single digits
3. **`use-dd-space-spelled-out-month-space-yyyy-and-use-spanish-word-septiembre-for-ninth-month`** - Dd space spelled out month space yyyy and spanish word septiembre for ninth month
4. **`use-spelled-out-month-space-dd-comma-space-yyyy-and-use-spanish-word-septiembre-for-ninth-month`** - Spelled out month space dd comma space yyyy and spanish word septiembre for ninth month
5. **`use-dd-space-spelled-out-month-space-yyyy-and-use-spanish-word-setiembre-for-ninth-month`** - Dd space spelled out month space yyyy and spanish word setiembre for ninth month

</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Dates In Numerical Form">
Control specific formatting options.

**Available options:**
1. **`use-dd-slash-mm-slash-yyyy`** - Day/Month/Year format (DD/MM/YYYY)
2. **`use-dd-hyphen-mm-hyphen-yyyy`** - Dd hyphen mm hyphen yyyy
3. **`use-dd-period-mm-period-yyyy`** - Day.Month.Year format (DD.MM.YYYY)

</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Time Format">
Choose between 12-hour and 24-hour clock formats.

**Available options:**
1. **`use-24-hour-clock-with-colon-as-separator`** - 24 hour clock with colon as separator
2. **`use-24-hour-clock-with-period-as-separator`** - 24 hour clock with period as separator
3. **`use-12-hour-clock-without-leading-zero-use-period-as-separator-and-lowercase-am-or-pm-with-periods-and-spaces`** - 12 hour clock without leading zero period as separator and lowercase am or pm with periods and spaces
4. **`use-12-hour-clock-without-leading-zero-or-minutes-for-full-hours-use-colon-as-separator-and-uppercase-am-or-pm-without-periods`** - 12 hour clock without leading zero or minutes for full hours colon as separator and uppercase am or pm without periods
5. **`use-12-hour-clock-without-leading-zero-or-minutes-for-full-hours-use-colon-as-separator-and-lowercase-am-or-pm-without-periods`** - 12 hour clock without leading zero or minutes for full hours colon as separator and lowercase am or pm without periods
6. **`spell-out-time-in-words`** - Spell out time in words

</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Language-Specific Information

Spanish varies significantly between regions (Spain, Latin America). Style rules help you choose the appropriate regional variant.

## Common Use Cases

- Latin American vs European Spanish variants
- Business communications (formal vs informal)
- Academic writing (formal register)
- Marketing content (regional appropriateness)
